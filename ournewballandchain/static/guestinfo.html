Air Travel

If you're flying into Georgia for our wedding, the closest airport to the ceremony, reception, hotels and attractios is undoubtedly Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport - with airport code (ATL). The Atlanta airport is south of the ceremony, reception and hotel block. Just a note: Atlanta is a driving-style city, so a vehicle in the form of rental car, car-pool with other visitors or taxis/car service will most likely be necessary. MARTA - Atlanta's subway system - is available and may be useful in certain circumstances, such as getting into Midtown Atlanta or Decatur, so we recommend our visitors research MARTA before taking it. Please see the MARTA website for more details.

Kate and Jonathan's Favorite Visitor Sights and Attractions - Things to Do

  • Fernbank Museum of Natural History : We almost decided to hold our wedding reception here! Dinosaur skeletons and all! FERNBANK Website
  • Atlanta Botanical Gardens : Simply gorgeous. Highly recommended! BOTANICAL GARDENS Website
  • Georgia Aquarium : The World's largest Aquarium. A great crowd-pleaser! AQUARIUM Website
  • High Museum of Art : Permanent Collection includes European Renaissance and Baroque Art, African Art, Photography, Folk Art, Decorative and Modern/Contemporary. At the time of our wedding, current exhibitions include Photography of Civil Rights, Contemporary art of Aaron Curry, and the role of nature in jewelry and homewares by Georgian artist Gogo. MUSEUM Website
  • Georgia Tech's Campus : Jonny's Alma Mater! Located just downtown, it's great for a bit of a walk. Our rambling wreck spent a lot of time at The Howie Building - School of Physics. Go, Yellow Jackets! GT Website
  • Michael C. Carlos Museum on Emory Campus : Art Museum on Emory University's Campus - collections include art of Africa, Asia, Egypt and Nubia, Greek and Roman (yay!) and the Americas. MUSEUM Website
  • Paris on Ponce : Shopping for antiques, curiosities, one-of-a-kind and locally made artistic treats. Big and small items - TONS of fun! Don't forget to look in at The Red Room event space... Le Maison Rouge is a (very red) homage to Paris's Moulin Rouge! PARIS ON PONCE Website

Kate and Jonathan's Recommendations to Visitors - Favorite Restaurants and Frequented Watering-Holes

  • San Francisco Coffee: Jonathan's favorite! Great for a caffinated pick-me up. SF Website
  • Fox Brothers BBQ : Kate's choice for BBQ! Relaxed atmosphere and tasty fare. Don't miss the Brisket, Pulled Pork, Cole Slaw, Mac'n'cheese, Brunswick stew, Chocolate Pecan Pie... so, basically don't miss any of it. It's not our biggest recommendation for vegetarians - mostly meats available. FOX BROS Website
  • Highland Bakery : Jonathan's #1 brunch spot. We love the home-cooked feel! Hoppin' Highland, Shrimp'n'Grits, Burrito de Huevos, Fried Chicken Benedict, yum. Three locations and bakeshops - Old 4th Ward, Midtown, and Buckhead. HIGHLAND Website
  • The Porter Beer Bar : This is a must-visit every time we're in Atlanta! Amazing beer list inlcuding local artisanal and good ol'favorites. A tasty food menu to compliments your brew of choice. The staff and servers here take their drink/food duties really seriously. Any questions, they'll help you out. We love The Porter. PORTER Website
  • The Bookhouse Pub : A cozy, tride'n'true pub. Inexpensive beer, some interesting cocktails. Recommended : Chili, Chorizo burger, Veggie Burger. BOOKHOUSE Website
  • Ria's Bluebird : A great brunch option!! Highly recommend. Full of cheeky character. Chef Ria won the Food Network program "Chopped"! BLUEBIRD Website
  • Mary Mac's Tea Room : Sweet and quite old-fashioned Southern Atlanta. Fried Chicken, real Sweet Tea, Pot Likker, Mint Juleps. According to one yelp.com reviewer, if you circle order "all" on your bread order, your meal starts with a cinnamon roll. TEA ROOM Website
  • The Midway Pub : A frequented East-Atlanta favorite. We don't know why, specifically. We just like it. :) Good people, good beer options, staples in pub-grub, a large amount of outdoor seating. A pleasant place to have a drink or two. MIDWAY Website
  • Farm Burger : Ok, so, there are tons of burger places in Atlanta. The pretty darn good ones are ""Yeah! Burger", "Flip Burger", and "Farm Burger"... the latter being our #1 choice. Less frills... a downright tasty burger. FARM BURGER Website
  • The Varsity : This is a Georgia Tech staple and self-celebrated as the "World's Largest Drive-in". Started in 1928, the fast-food joint boasts of 1950's decor complete with car hops and limited diner-style fast food. Chili cheese dogs, slaw dogs, orange shakes and onion rings. Will not break the bank! :) VARSITY Website
  • EATS : Just like it sounds. FOOD. And lots of it. On the cheap. Nice. EATS Website
